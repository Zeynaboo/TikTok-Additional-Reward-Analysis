# Findings

This file summarizes the current dashboard findings in a portfolio-friendly format. The wording is intentionally cautious: the project identifies patterns associated with monetization outcomes, not deterministic rules for the TikTok algorithm.

## Finding 1

Statement:

- `Additional Reward` is concentrated in a small subset of the sample rather than being broadly distributed across all videos.

Evidence:

- current dashboard sample: `80` matched videos
- rewarded video count: `6`
- total additional reward: `$31.81`

Business implication:

- the practical goal is not just to increase views, but to identify the conditions under which a video moves from standard monetization into a smaller high-value rewarded group.

## Finding 2

Statement:

- reward activation appears more often alongside stronger watch time and stronger search contribution than in the broader non-rewarded group.

Evidence:

- the `Reward Drivers` page shows rewarded outliers clustered at higher watch-time levels than most non-rewarded videos
- the `Search and SEO` page shows rewarded and search-led videos recurring around stronger search exposure zones

Business implication:

- content strategy should prioritize videos that keep viewers longer and are likely to capture intentional search behavior, rather than relying only on broad viral reach.

## Finding 3

Statement:

- MBTI pairing, relationship, and curiosity-driven query phrasing recur frequently among top search-led videos.

Evidence:

- the `Content Strategy` and `Search and SEO` pages repeatedly surface search-led videos with query themes such as MBTI pairings, direct relationship framing, and "why/what type" curiosity prompts
- the current dashboard average search traffic is `15.38%`

Business implication:

- future testing should continue around high-intent search phrasing, especially topics that connect personality type combinations with conflict, compatibility, or explanation-driven hooks.

## Finding 4

Statement:

- the account's reach is heavily discovery-led rather than follower-led.

Evidence:

- current audience page metrics show approximately `89.59%` non-followers and `10.41%` followers
- the same page shows `18.23%` new viewers on average in the current dashboard

Business implication:

- the content system should be optimized for searchable, cold-audience-friendly packaging rather than assuming an existing follower base will drive monetization performance.

## Finding 5

Statement:

- the core audience in the current dashboard is concentrated in the `18-34` range, with `25-34` as the largest age segment.

Evidence:

- age summary cards currently show:
  - `18-24`: `35%`
  - `25-34`: `45%`
  - `35-44`: `13%`

Business implication:

- packaging, hooks, and examples should stay aligned with an adult discovery audience rather than a teen-first framing.

## Limits

- the sample is still moderate and should be treated as exploratory
- the dashboard is strongest for reward, search, and retention patterns; geography and some demographic detail remain more reliable as support views than headline proof
- current findings identify associations, not causal mechanisms
